UF Digital Arts and Sciences Club

Role: Founder, President

Duration: October 2016-Present


In the Fall of 2016 I started the UF Digital Arts and Sciences Club (UF DAS). UF DAS is one of ten Special Interest Groups of UF's Association for Computing Machinery chapter

As President, I help plan events and develop the club. This has been (and continues to be) a great learning experience in running an organization.

Spring 2017 was UFDAS's first real semester as a club. We held a single general body meeting at the beginning of the semester to help spread awareness of our new club. Our recurring events throughout the semester was a tutorial series on Autodesk Maya. We also cohosted several events with some other organizations: a DAS Townhall with the CISE department, a Supplement with First Time Programmers (another ACM SIG), and CodeCase (a showcase of projects worked on by clubs and indivduals throughout the semester) with ACM as a whole.
