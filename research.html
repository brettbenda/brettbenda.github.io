Research Overview

My current research focuses on human perception of hand placement and movement in virtual reality. Previously, I have worked on projects involving multimodal interactions with virtual agents and attitudes towards biometric authentication with smart home devices.



Current Projects


Investigation of Perceptible Scaled and Offset Hand Placement in VR

This research seeks to identify ranged of acceptable values for scaled and offset hand movements before they are perceptible to users in VR. Simple cases as well as cognitively complex scenarios are examined to determine how task complexity affects human perception.

Related Publications

Previous Projects


Visualization of Drone Flight Data for Pilot Education

This project investigated the presentation of drone flight data in order to train novice drone pilots best practices and strategies for drone piloting during building inspection tasks.

The development and evaluation of this system was in-part conducted as part of CIS6930 Information Visualization during the Fall 2019 semester.
